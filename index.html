How is this calculated?

We use a linear model based on observed real-world data from competitive shooters and published powder testing:

velocity_change = ΔT × (sensitivity / 20) × (base_velocity / 100)

Sensitivity values (% velocity change per 20°F drop) come from aggregated data across multiple sources including Vihtavuori, Accurate, and large-scale chrono testing by practical shooters:

  • 1.9% — Fast powders (Titegroup, Bullseye)
  • 1.6% — Most common 9mm competition powders (N320, Sport Pistol)
  • 1.3% — Medium powders (Unique, Power Pistol)
  • 1.0% — Conservative default for unknown powders

This is a practical estimate, not a laboratory measurement. Actual velocity shift depends on powder lot, primer, case volume, and your specific gun. Always verify with your chrono in match conditions when possible.

Limitations & How to Use

This tool is designed to help you prepare and validate loads before a match. It is not a replacement for chronographing your ammo at the actual match conditions.

Temperature estimates are based on real-world data from competitive shooters but are still approximations. Different powder lots can behave differently.

Best practice: Chrono your loads in the gun you will compete with, in similar temperatures to the match, and build in a safety margin.

All calculations happen in your browser. No data is sent anywhere.
